Synthesis Notes

We assume five processes that result in nasal-oral alternations: (1) leftward spreading LDNH, affecting all vowels and sonorants within the root and a potential series of prefixes;(2) LO, whereby /N/ --> [ND] / _V; (3) Morphophonological syllabic LN whereby 3 specified suffixes (-ramo, -rani, -ma) nasalize the preceding root-final vowel; (4) A morphophonological process whereby two specific suffixes (-ɲo, -rã) initiate LDNH (as opposed to a root-internal trigger); (5) Morphophonological syllabic LN whereby the postposition téi and suffix -tei are nasalized by a root-final nasal vowel.
