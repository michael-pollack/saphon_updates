Synthesis Notes
We adopt Borges' analysis of Avá-Canoeiro Goiás, given that it is the only description for this variety of Avá-Canoeiro. Following Borges' analysis, we assume a series of 3 nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ and a series of six vowels that contrast in orality-nasality /i, ɨ, e, a, o, u/, and adopt 4 (morpho)-phonological processes: (1) bidirectional LN, optional nasalization of /ɾ/ to [ɾ̃] before or after a nasal vowel, primarily in stressed syllables; (2) bidirectional LN, obligatory nasalization of glides /w, j/ to [w̃], [j̃] ~ [ɲ], respectively, before or after a nasal vowel, primarily in stressed syllables; (3) leftward LN, nasalization of oral vowels before nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/; (4) rightward MPP, nasalization of the locative suffix {-pe} to {-m} after a nasal vowel in the previous morpheme. We differ from Borges' analysis in proposing an additional process of leftward LDNH, where a nasal vowel or nasal consonant triggers nasalization of sonorants, including oral vowels and the approximants /ɾ, w, j/, with voiceless obstruents (p, t, tʃ, k, kʷ) acting as blockers. We assume LDNH to occur in the Goiás variety of Avá-Canoeiro for the following reason: transcriptions in Borges' analysis for local nasalization processes result in surface forms which cannot be derived solely from local rules, for example: /kujã/ → [qʊ̃'ɲə̃ː] ~ [qʊ̃'j̃ə̃ː] 'mulher', /o-jeŋ/ → [õ'ɲẽːŋə] ~ [õ'j̃ẽːŋə] 'ele está falando', /i-waŋ/ → [ɪ'w̃ə̃ːŋə] 'é vermelho', /kuɾum/ → [qʊ'ɾ̃ũːm] ~ [qʊ'ɾũːm] 'menino' (p. 85); /wama/ → ['w̃ə̃ːmə] 'pasto', /o-momew/ → [ˌõmõˈmeːw] 'ele conversou' (p. 90).
