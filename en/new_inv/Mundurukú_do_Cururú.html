Synthesis Notes
We largely follow Braun & Crofts' (1965) description, incorporating certain elements of Crofts (1985). Both sources document a process which spreads nasalization from suffixes to roots. We follow Crofts (1985:xiv) in considering this process LDNH, spreading through the word until reaching an opaque obstruent. This latter restriction seems to require that all other consonants /ʔ d s ʃ h w j ɾ/ are transparent. A datum (The word for 'soldados', [sõ̀ɾã̄ɾã̄-jũ̀], xiv) supports the claim that /ɾ/ is transparent. The datum [ōʔã̀-jũ̀] 'machado' (xiv) seems to show that /ʔ/ actually is opaque. However, we believe from context that this is in error. the prose of the paragraph ("toda a palavra tornou-se nasal.... p. ex:", xiv) sets these examples up to show full transparency, and it is typologically common for glottals to be transparent. It is also odd that /d/ is not listed among the opaque obstruents, given all the other voiced obstruents are. On the other hand, /d/ exhibits strange behavior different from other obstruents in the Pará dialect documented extensively in Picanço 2005, so this gap is credible, allowing us to place /d/ among the transparent segments with some confidence.
