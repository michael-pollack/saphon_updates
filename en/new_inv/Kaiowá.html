Synthesis Notes
We largely follow Cardoso's analysis of nasality in Kaiowá, though add specific details relation to morpho(phono)logical properties of the language that she does not discuss. Specifically, we follow Harrison & Taylor's description of nasal-oral allomorphy process. We assume that there are other suffixes that pattern with {py}, though none of the authors list them explicitly. The nasal span in a morpheme has at its right edge a nasal segment, and the nasal span runs all the way to the left edge of a prosodic word boundary, including all potential prefixes. When a voiced stop is wholly contained inside a nasal span, it surfaces as fully nasal (e.g. [m]); when it is wholly contained inside an oral span, it surfaces as fully oral (e.g. [b]); and when it appears at the boundary between a nasal and an oral span, its surfaces as partially nasal (e.g. [mb]).
