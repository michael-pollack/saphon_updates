Synthesis Notes

This synthesis mainly follows Leite's (1977) phonological description of Tapirapé, with further details regarding process drawn from her 2003 article. Following Leite's (1977) analysis, we consider Tapirapé to have a series of 3 nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ and a series of 5 vowels, /i, ɨ, ɛ, a, o/, which contrast in orality/nasality. The phonological inventory is taken from Leite's (1977) MA thesis, given that it is more detailed compared to her 2003 article. Following Leite, (1977, 2003) we consider 8 (morpho)-phonological processes related to orality/nasality to occur in Tapirapé: (1) circumdirectional LN, nasalization of tap between two nasal vowels (1977:8); (2) rightward MPP, the causative prefix /ma-/ triggers obligatory nasalization of root-initial /p, k/ to [m, ŋ] in the following roots: {ket, pat, kãto, poko} (2003:58); (3) rightward LN, syllable coda /j/ nasalizes to [j̃] after a nasal vowel (1977:8-9); (4) rightward LN, in a post-tonic syllable /j/ nasalizes to [ɲ] after a nasal vowel (1977:8-9); (5) leftward MPP, in a root-suffix combination, roots ending in /am, an/ surface as [ãw, ãɾ] before the suffixes {-ã.NMLZ, -i.NEG, -ãw+a.CIRC, -ã.GER} (1977:11-14); (6) leftward LN, weak nasalization of oral vowels before a nasal consonant (1977:5-6); (7) leftward MPP, nasalization of the 3.SG prefix /a/ to [ã] before a nasal consonant (2003:52); (8) rightward MPP, in a root-suffix combination initial /p, k/ in the suffixes {patan, poko, pam, kãto} optionally nasalize to [matan, moko, mam, ŋãto] when the preceding root ends in one of the following sequences: /Vm, Vn, Ṽj, Vŋ, Ṽ/ (1977:12-13).
