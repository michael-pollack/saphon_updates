Synthesis Notes
We mainly adopt da Silva's (2009) analysis for Kuruayá, supplemented with processes from Picanço (2005, 2010). Following da Silva we assume underlying nasal consonants (N), voiced stops (D), and a two-way contrast between oral (V) and nasal (Ṽ) vowels. We also adopt the tone inventory from Costa's (2002) MA thesis given that it is the only source from those included above which explicitedly discusses tone for which we assume two tones: high and low. Tone has been included with following notation: 5 for high tone and 1 for low tone. Following da Silva's analysis, we assume (1) a process of bidirectional LN, in which /ɾ, w, l, j/ are nasalized before or after a nasal vowel. We adopt Picanço's analysis for (2) leftward LDNH, triggered by the right-most nasal vowel and spreading its nasality leftward, nasalizing oral vowels and approximants /ɾ, w, j, l/. However, we assume that /p, t, k, tʃ, s, ʃ/ block nasal harmony while /ʔ, h/ are transparent to this process.
