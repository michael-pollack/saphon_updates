Synthesis Notes

Our analysis differs from that of the above authors in the following ways: We assume a process of LO, whereby /m, n/ --> [mb, nd] before oral vowels, only in prosodically strong positions and for the Tocantins variety. We also assume a process of leftward LDNH affecting all vowels and sonorants. LDNH is not described fro Avá-Canoeiro, but both authors talk about local vowel nasalization and local sonorant nasalization processes. These local processes are insufficient to derive the long nasal spans present in the author's data, and looks very much like LDNH in Tapirape, as described by de Freitas Leite. We assume LDNH to occur in the Tocantins variety of Avá-Canoeiro for the following reason: transcriptions in Borges' analysis for local nasalization processes result in in surface forms which cannot be derived solely from local rules, for example: /kujã/ → [qʊ̃'ɲə̃ː] ~ [qʊ̃'j̃ə̃ː] 'mulher', /o-jeŋ/ → [õ'ɲẽːŋə] ~ [õ'j̃ẽːŋə] 'ele está falando', /i-waŋ/ → [ɪ'w̃ə̃ːŋə] 'é vermelho', /kuɾum/ → [qʊ'ɾ̃ũːm] ~ [qʊ'ɾũːm] 'menino' (p. 85); /wama/ → ['w̃ə̃ːmə] 'pasto', /o-momew/ → [ˌõmõˈmeːw] 'ele conversou' (p. 90).
