Synthesis Notes
This synthesis mainly adoptes Alves' (2008) analysis, with minor differences. We consider /b/ to be phonemic based on the author's claim that it appears in one data point [bɨdɛ] '1.PL'. We differ from Alves' analysis is that we consider a process of leftward LDNH that is triggered either by root-final nasal consonants or a root-final nasal vowel, rather than just triggered by a nasal vowel. We also consider all voiceless obstruents (T) and /h/ to be transparent to the harmony process, rather than just /ʔ, h/. We also assume a BN process in which /j/ is nasalized to [ɲ] root-initially.
