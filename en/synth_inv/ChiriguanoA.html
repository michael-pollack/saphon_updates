Synthesis Notes

We assume five processes that result in nasal-oral alternations: (1) leftward spreading LDNH triggered by nasal vowels and nasal stops, affecting all vowels and sonorants within the root and a potential series of prefixes; (2) LO, whereby /N/ --> [ND] / _V; (3) morphophonological LN, whereby /T/ --> [ND] / mõ+_; (4) Morphophonological syllabic LN whereby 3 specified suffixes (-ramo, -rani, -ma) nasalize the preceding root-final vowel; (5) A morphophonological process whereby two specific suffixes (-ɲo, -rã) initiate LDNH (as opposed to a root-internal trigger).
