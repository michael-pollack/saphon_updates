Synthesis Notes

We mostly adopt Rodrigues' (1958) analysis, but our account differs in some crucial ways. (1) We construe the data that Rodrigues and Lemos Barbosa present as providing evidence of LDNH in the language. (2) We treat the palatal alternation between [j ~ ɲ] as resulting from the oralization of /ɲ/, thereby mirroring the pattern of oralization for ther other nasal consonants. (3) We also analyze /β/ as being the underlying phoneme, doing away with a single voiced stop.
