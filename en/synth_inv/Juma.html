Synthesis Notes

We follow Abrahamson & Abrahamson in positing full underlying nasal stops that post-oralize in all position of the words when followed by oral vowels. Nasal consonants pre-oralize after an oral vowel, but only in root-dinal position, e.g. [pirarubm] 'fish eggs' p. 8; pre-oralized consonants are not observed elsewhere in the word. Under our analysis, this latter pattern is due to the fact that vowels in the final syllable of roots/morphemes exhibit absolute faithfulness. This means that these final vowels when underlying oral, do not nasalize, even when followed by a root-final underlying nasal consonant. Instead, such vowels remain oral and pre-oralize the final nasal consonant. The language exhibits a phonemic /g/, which can appear adjacent to oral and nasal vowels without affecting them, e.g. [gãmãipó] 'he delayed much'(p. 5). We adopt Abrahamson's LN proposal that the suffixes {-mɨ̃, -nɛ̃he} nasalize the final oral vowel of the preceding morpheme (1980:22) but posit that they don't trigger the de-post-oralization of [ND], e.g. [ɔ̃mõmbɨ̃nɛ̃hɛ]. Sequences of the type [N... ND], [N ... N], [ND ... DN] are common in the data, but sequences of the type [ND ... N], which are otherwise unattested in TG are observed in a few examples (1984, p.9). We have doubts about these data, as did the editor/translator of the paper, who specifically remarks upon them as unusual, unexplained, and inconsistent with the rest of Abrahamson & Abrahamson's analysis. Sequences of the type [ND ... ND], [ND ... Ṽ] (with the exception of the structures derived by the presence of the above-noted suffixes), [DN ... ND], and [DN ... N] are not attested at all. With these caveats then, fully nasal and post-oralized nasals are in complementary distribution in the data: [N] occurs before nasal vowels, whereas [ND] occurs before oral vowels. Based on these facts, we infer that Júma exhibits a restricted process of nasal harmony, which spreads leftward from a root-final nasal vowel through /V, w, j/. The data is sufficiently inconsistent that it is not possible for us to reach reliable conclusions regarding which segments are blockers and which are transparent, and whether prefix-root boundaries are likewise opaque or transparent.
