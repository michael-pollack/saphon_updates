Synthesis Notes

This synthesis draws mainly on Pereira's (2009) dissertation, which provides the only detailed description of nasal/oral alternations for Asurini do Xingú. Following Pereira we posit a series of phonemically nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ and a five vowel qualities /i, ɨ, e, a, o, u/ which contrast in orality and nasality. We consider the following processes to occur in Asurini do Xingú, as per Pereira's original claims: (1) circumdirectional LO, optional oralization of /m, n/ to [m] ~ [b], [n] ~ [d] between two oral vowels; (2) leftward LO, optional oralization of word-initial /m, n/ to [m] ~ [b], [n] ~ [d] before an oral vowel; (3) leftward LN, nasalization of syllable onset /j/ to [ɲ] before a nasal vowel. We diverge from Pereira's analysis in the following ways: (1) We consider /ŋ/ to be an underlyinɡ phoneme and do not consider its allophone [ɡ] to be the elsewhere case, as this distribution seems improbable; (2) we consider /ŋ/ to also underɡo optional oralization to [ŋ] ~ [ɡ] before an oral vowel and between two oral vowels, consistent with the oralization processes of /m, n/ → [m] ~ [b], [n] ~ [d] proposed by Pereira. Based on the data provided by Pereira, we believe these two processes (leftward LO and circumdirectional LO) to be restricted to prefixes and roots: [ʔa'ga] 'namorado', ['ga] 'ele', [a'gu] 'eu cuido' (2009:66); (3) we propose that nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ obligatorily post-oralize to [mb, nd, ŋɡ], respectively, between a nasal consonant and an oral vowel. We do not consider Pereira's claim that these consonants surface as "pre-nasal" to be possible.
