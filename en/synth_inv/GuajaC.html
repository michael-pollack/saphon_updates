Synthesis Notes

Our analysis is significantly informed by Nascimento 2008, and we propose the following rules: (1) leftward LDNH triggered by the rightmost stressed nasal Ṽ, with oral vowels and glides as undergoers, obstruents as blockers, and glottals as transparent; (2) bidirectional nasalization of vowels adjacent to nasal consonants; (3) nasal oral allomorphy for some morphemes when in the presence of another morpheme containing a nasal consonant, with two examples given below. Regarding #2, we note that Cunha divides this process into two: (i) nasalization of oral V when preceding nasal m,n; (ii) nasalization of oral V when following word initial m,n; however, we assume that these two procesesses are actually just part of a more general processes of LN:V.
