Synthesis Notes

We adopt Gabas' (1999) analysis of Karo. Following Gabas' analysis, we assume a series of 3 nasals /m, n, ŋ/ and a two-way contrast between oral and nasal vowels, along with 8 processes: (1) rightward local syllabic nasalization, a nasal vowel in the penultimate stressed syllable spreads its nasality rightward to the following onset, nasalizing /ɾ, ɡ/ to [ɾ̃, ŋ], then nasalizing the following oral vowel in the nucleus; (2) bidirectional LN, optional nasalization of vowels between a tautosyllabic nasal onset and a heterosyllabic nasal onset belonging to a following stressed syllable; (3) leftward LO, post-oralization of /m, n, ŋ/ in a stressed syllable onset before an oral vowel; (4) rightward LO, shielding/pre-oralization of word-final /m, n, ŋ/ to [bm, dn, ɡŋ] after oral vowel nuclei in stressed syllables; (5) bidirectional LN, nasalization of glides /w, j/ when adjacent to a nasal vowel; (6) rightward LN, nasalization of morpheme-initial /p, t, k/ to [m, n, ŋ] after final nasal consonants in the preceding word; (7) circumdirectional LN, nasalization of word-final /p, t, k/ in syllable codas when between a nasal vowel nucleus and an initial nasal consonant in the following word; (8) rightward MPP, nasalization of the indicative suffix {-p} to [-m] after verb roots with final nasal vowels.
