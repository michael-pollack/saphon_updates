Synthesis Notes

We adopt Sandy & O'Hagan's (2020) description of San Joaquín de Omaguas Omagua. Following Sandy & O'Hagan, we posit a series of 3 nasals for Omagua /m, n, ɴ/ with the final consonant being a placeless nasal, along with a series of 4 oral vowels /i, ɪ, u a/. The San Joaquín de Omaguas variety of Omagua has lost nasal vowels, different from the Kambeba variety. We adopt Sandy & O'Hagan's analysis in assuming one phonological process: (1) bidirectional LN, an underspecified, placeless nasal /ɴ/ deletes and surfaces as a supersegment {~}. This supersegment then docks onto the preceding vowel and nasalizes it, with optional nasalization of the following surface glide /i/ --> [j].
