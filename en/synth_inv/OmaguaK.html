Synthesis Notes

We generally follow dos Santos' analysis of Omagua, with some minor alternations. First, we assume that the optional LN processes whereby all vowels are nasalized before a nasal consonant applies to all vowel qualities, rather than only /e, u/, since the restriction these vowel qualities seems difficult to justify. Second, we also treat /d/ as contrastive, along with /b, g/, since it presents the same distribution as the other contrastive voiced stops.
