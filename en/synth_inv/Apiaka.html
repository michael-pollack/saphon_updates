Synthesis Notes

This synthesis relies on Padua's (2007) thesis given that it is the only description available for Apiaká. We follow Padua's claims in assuming a series of 3 nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ and a series of vowels which contrast for orality and nasality. Of the processes proposed by Padua, we only adopt one: (1) leftward LN, optional nasalization of /j/ to either [j] or [ɲ] before a nasal vowel, resulting in the following "free variation": [j] ~ [ɲ] (p. 28). This synthesis is informed by Padua's transcriptions which lead us to propose a different generalization for the distribution of [N] ~ [ND] ~ [D]. We propose 4 processes for Apiaká: (1) leftward LN, optional nasalization of /j/ to [j] ~ [ɲ] before a nasal vowel, in variation with each other; (2) leftward LO, post-oralization of nasal consonants /m, n, ŋ/ to [mb, nd, ŋɡ] before an oral vowel in a stressed syllable; (3) leftward LO, oralization of /m, n, ŋ/ word-initially before an oral vowel; (4) leftward LDNH, a stressed root-final nasal vowel spreads its nasality leftward, nasalizing all sonorants (oral vowels and approximants /ɾ, w, j/). Our analysis differs from Padua's in the following ways: (1) we re-interpret Padua's environment of nasal consonants oralizing "following silence" (p. 29-30) as word-initially before an oral vowel, based on the following transcriptions: /mósa/ [ˈbɔsa] 'cobra', /muɾiʃiwá/ [buɾisiˈwa] 'buriti', /neréra/ [deˈɾɛɾa] 'teu nome', /nepɨ́a/ [deˈpɨɐ] 'teu pé' (p. 29-30); (2) we subsume Padua's post-oralization of /n/ to [nd] under a general post-oralization process, given that the author's environments in his transcriptions for the surfacing of [nd] are identical to his transcriptions for surface [mb] and [ŋɡ]. All undergoers appear in a stressed syllable before an oral vowel: /kwanoa/ [kwanˈdoa] 'gavião'; /sinuné neʔẽ/ [sinunˈdɛ deʔẽ] 'à frente'; /jakunaʔí/ [jakunˈdaˈʔi] 'jacundá' (p. 30) as compared to /si nɨmía/ [si nə̃mˈbiɐ] 'minha orelha' (p. 29), /wajnɨmú/ [wajnə̃mˈbu] 'beija-flor' (p. 29) and /tukãŋéɾa/ [tukãŋˈɡɛɾa] 'esp. de formiga' (p. 31).
