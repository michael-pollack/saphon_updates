Synthesis Notes

We adopt dos Santos' analysis of nasality-related (morpho-)phonological processes in Uru-eu-wau-wau. Specifically, we take the LDNH and MPP:V processes from the manuscript "Blocking effects of flap and glottal stop in nasal harmony processes in Kawahiva (Tupi-Guarani)", and the MPP:mb, LO and LN processes from the "Notas sobre o povo e língua Kawahiva: com referência especial ao(s) Uru Eu Wau Wau" manuscript. Following dos Santos, Uru-eu-wau-wau has a series of phonemic nasal consonants and a series of nasal vowels. (1) LDNH: Root-final nasal vowels trigger leftward LDNH to all /V, w, j/ within a root (but never extends beyond a root boundary), glottal stop and flap are opaque, and /T, h/ are transparent; (2) rightward MPP:mb: nasalization of oral vowel and pre-nasalization of /p, t, k/ to [mp, nt, ŋɡ] when the preceding morpheme ends in a nasal consonant, a nasal vowel, or when preceded by the causative prefix {mõ-}; (3) leftward LO, post-oralization of nasals /m, n, ŋ/ to [mb, nd, ŋɡ] before an oral vowel; (4) leftward LN, nasalization of an oral vowel when preceding a nasal consonant; (5) rightward MPP:V, whereby some suffixes with initial oral vowel (e.g. /-a, -uhu/) nasalize when previous root ends in a nasal consonant or nasal V.
