Synthesis Notes

This synthesis closely follows Thomas' (2014) analysis of Mbyá given that it is the only description for the variety spoken in Argentina. Following Thomas, we consider Mbyá to have contrastive oral and nasal vowels. We adopt the following processes from Thomas: (1) rightward MPP, nasalization of the locative {-pɨ} and diminutive suffix {-ʔi} to [mɨ̃] and [ʔĩ], respectively, after a root-final nasal vowel. In this process the glottal stop /ʔ/ from the diminutive suffix is not nasalized; (2) leftward LDNH, a root-final stressed nasal vowel spreads its nasalization, nasalizing oral vowels and the voiced segments /ɾ, w, ɡʷ, β, dʒ/. Oral stops /p, t, tʃ, k, kʷ, ʔ/ act as transparent to this process; (3) rightward MPP, in a root-suffix compound a root-final nasal vowel triggers pre-nasalization of oral stops /p, t, k, kʷ/ to [mb, nd, ŋɡ, ŋɡʷ], respectively. We diverge from Thomas' analysis in the following ways: (1) we consider nasal consonants to be underlying and undergo a process of post-oralization before an oral vowel, i.e. /m, n, ŋ, ŋʷ/ to [mb, nd, ŋɡ, ŋɡʷ], respectively (leftward LO). We infer that this process occurs in prefixes, roots, and suffixes, based on the following data points from Thomas: kũɲã+ŋɡʷe 'woman', õ+mãnõ+mba 'they all died', ɲãnẽ+mbaraka 'our guitar' (p. 84-85).
