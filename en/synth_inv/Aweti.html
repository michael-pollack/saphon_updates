Synthesis Notes

We mostly adopt Drude's analysis and phonemic inventory for Awetí, supplementing processes from Monserrat as well. We differ from Drude's analysis in which we consider Awetí to have underlying voiceless obstruents (T) and nasal consonants (N), as opposed to the 3-way contrast between voiceless obstruents, nasal consonants, and underspecified root-final arciphonemes proposed by Drude. We also assume Awetí has contrastive oral (V) and nasal vowels (Ṽ) and do not consider neutral vowels to occur in Awetí. We adopt 4 phonological processes: (1) leftward LDNH, triggered by root-final nasal vowels or nasal consonants, spreading leftward and nasalizing all oral vowels and approximants; voiceless obstruents {T} and /h/ are considered transparent to this process; (2) rightward LN, pre-nasalization of /p, t, ts, k/ to [mp, nt, ŋk, nts] ~ [nb, nd, ŋɡ] following a nasal vowel; (3) bidirectional LN, nasalization of the glides /w, j/ preceding or following a nasal vowel. /j/ contains two nasal allophones, which are in variation with each other: [j̃] ~ [ɲ]; (4) bidirectional LN, deletion of the nasal portion /m/ in the emphatic suffix {-mɛ} which triggers nasalization of the preceding root-final stops /p, t, k/ to [m, n, ŋ] as well as nasalization of the following /ɛ/ vowel in the suffix.
