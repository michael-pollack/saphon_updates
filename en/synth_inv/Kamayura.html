Synthesis Notes

We mostly follow Seki's description of Kamayurá, which is corroborated and supplemented by the descriptions offered by Salzer & Silva as well. The one place that we diverge from Seki and da Silva's phonological analysis, and converge with Salzer's, is in positing a /ɲ/ phoneme. While most surface [ɲ] can be accounted for as the outcome of leftward LDNH operating on /j/, this is not the case for word-final [ɲ]. The analytical alternatives are either to posit underlying /ɲ/ in such cases; or to posit a LN process that operates rightward from an underlying nasal vowel in just this case. It is not clear from the data whether all cases of word-final [ɲ] are in fact immediately preceded by an underlying nasal vowel, however, leading us to take the more-surface transparent option of positing underlying /ɲ/ in these cases. We adopt 4 (morpho)phonological processes giving rise to nasal-oral alternations: (1) LDNH, triggered by root-final nasal vowels, spreading leftward to all sonorants within the root and prefixe(es), and transparent to all obstruents; (2) LN triggered by root-final nasal consonants, affecting immediately preceding vowels. We follow Seki & Silva in assuming that LN of vowels is only triggered by word-final nasal consonants, but we note that Salzer assumes a more general process whereby vowels adjacent to any nasal consonant are nasalized. We also consider two different MPP processes: (3) rightward MPP, in which root-initial {p, k} of lexically specified roots nasalize to [m, ŋ] following the causative prefix {mo-}; and (4) leftward MPP, in which the relative prefix {ɾ-} nasalizes to [n] when the preceding morpheme contains a nasal vowel.
