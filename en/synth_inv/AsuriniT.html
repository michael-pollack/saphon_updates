Synthesis Notes

We mostly base this analysis on Nicholson & Harrison's work. Asuriní doesn't have contrastive nasal vowels, and doesn't have any phonological processes involving nasality. However, it does exhibit a number of morphophonological alternations affecting some consonants that undergo oralization or nasalization in the presence of certain affixes or lexically specified roots. Following Nicholson: (1) LO - {m, n, ŋ} go to {p, t, k} in certain verb roots; (2) LO - {m, n} go to {w, ɾ} in certain roots; (3) LO - {m, n, ŋ} go to {p, h, k} in certain roots. Following Harrison: (1) LO /N/ --> [T] before -ta; (2) LN /T/ --> [N] after mo-; (3) LO /n/ --> [w] after mo-. Asuriní lost contrastive nasal vowels, and therefore LDNH.
