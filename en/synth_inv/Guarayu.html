Synthesis Notes

This summary is mostly based on Danielsen's analysis. Though the analysis is often not detailed enough to fully understand the specific facts of Guarayu, all of the description and data suggest that Guarayu exhibits the same nasal-oral alternations that are frequently observed in TG languages. As such, while the details of the empirical facts are not fully clear, this summary is based on the available data, and our knowledge of the common typological patterns in TG, with which the available Guarayu data is fully compatible. Note that in the Urubichá variety of Guarayu, stress has generally shifted to the penult, without the position of contrastive nasal vowels shifting from the historical position of stress, i.e. final syllable. In Urubichá, then, it is no longer stressed nasal vowels that are contrastive, but simply root-final nasal vowels.
